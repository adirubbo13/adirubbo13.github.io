Learning Outcomes by Track

Artificial Intelligence Track

I chose to pursue my master's degree because I wanted to stay on top of current topics in computer science. The rise of ChatGPT demonstrated the revolutionary nature of large language models. I wanted to understand how they worked, not just use them.

Key Courses and Applications:

  • Applied Machine Learning: Learned statistics and mathematical foundations behind predictive modeling. Strengthened understanding of how models learn from data and generalize to new inputs.
  • Natural Language Processing: Studied the fundamental objective of LLMs - predicting the next token in a sequence. Clarified how complex systems are built on structured probabilistic principles.
  • Deep Learning: Exposed to neural network architectures that compose modern large language models.
  • Human Centered AI Applications: Learned how to efficiently integrate AI powered tools into real technology stacks.

Data Pipelines and Platforms Track

During my undergraduate studies, I became interested in database design and the possibilities that arise from structured data systems. My education at the iSchool provided the foundation for building complete data pipelines.

Key Courses and Applications:

  • Database Management and Administration: Strengthened foundation in relational systems and optimization techniques.
  • Data Warehousing and Big Data: Expanded understanding of distributed systems and large scale processing. Learned that small architectural details significantly impact performance.

These lessons directly supported my two co-op experiences. At CooperVision, I supported large-scale data pipelines and governance processes. At Tessy Corporation, I designed JSON-driven workflow engines connecting frontend interfaces to operational logic.
